Word and Way

Knowledge without service is incomplete. You stand on the shoulders of thousands — and owe the next generation the same.

The Magaambya teaches a blend of arcane and primal traditions, but its deeper ethic is service: study must become action for all people and the natural world. Isolationist scholarship — hoarding magic for personal power — is the antithesis of Jatembe’s mission.

Old-Mage Jatembe

Folk hero of the Mwangi Expanse — wizard, teacher, myth. Credited with rekindling magic after Earthfall and the Age of Anguish, when civilization’s lore was ash.

  • First rumors: ~−3600 AR — “an old man, even in the oldest legends.”
  • Method: Stole magic from foul creatures and banished them; good things of the world gave him secrets.
  • Legacy: Founded Nantambu and the Magaambya (~−3502 AR); sightings continued for centuries, then he effectively vanished.
  • Book 5–6: Imprisoned aboard the King’s airship Mandibles of Fate on Akiton; PCs free him; appoints them new Magic Warriors.

What Jatembe is not (Book 1 fable)

A bad-luck spirit asked villagers where Jatembe kept his riches, spouses, weapons, children. They answered: no riches, no spouses, no weapons — his weapon was knowledge in his head. But he had many children: the students of Nantambu. The spirit turned on them instead.

Read aloud or paraphrase — foreshadows gremlins at Introduction Ceremony. Kindled Magic p. 8.

Ten Magic Warriors

Jatembe challenged his apprentices to surrender their names and wear golden animal masks — taking new identities in service. They reintroduced wizardry after Earthfall, advised communities, and fought evils such as the King of Biting Ants.

The school today

  • Oldest magic academy in the world — neutral good institution; evil PCs poor fit.
  • Head: High Sun-Mage Oyamba (Tempest-Sun); five Learned Ones lead branches.
  • Campus: Island in Nantambu’s canals; ten terraced towers; terraced gardens; free dining hall; Powderpile shop; Spire Dorm for new cohorts.
  • Demographics: Adults (rare children); global students; cohort model — PCs are one peer group, not a mercenary party.
  • Spellcasting: All PCs gain druid or wizard multiclass archetype at 2nd level (Player's Guide).
  • Five branches — Cascade Bearers (imagination), Emerald Boughs (camaraderie), Rain-Scribes (adaptability), Tempest-Sun (valor), Uzunjati (knowledge/story).

Anadi & the Magaambya

Janatimo’s fable: three anadi teachers revealed hybrid forms to Jade Feather on eve of acceptance; she agreed to discretion so they would not be driven off. Faculty have quietly spread helpful-spider stories for millennia — accepting anadi before the world was safe.

Spoken on the Song Wind Mwangi Folktales; see NOTES.md anadi section.

Hidden war (AP through-line)

Koride Ulawa holds the Vesicant Egg (King of Biting Ants’ psyche fragments) in a hidden lab — she does not know its true origin. It draws insects, gremlins, and eventually anadi toward campus. Serpentfolk seek it (Book 2+). Book 5: Doorway and Akiton. Book 6: egg hatches; Magic Warrior ceremony; colossal larva.

Iobane — Zenj magi order, spiritual successors to the Ten; guard the Doorway vs. undead Throat-of-Nothingness remnants.

“I sing the song of the one, the Old Mage… the Ten, the Magic Warriors… the hundreds who built this school… the thousands who came before us. Their strength lives on in me. Now they shall live on in you as well.”

Snake (adaptability), leopard (valor), spider (camaraderie), hyena (imagination), elephant (memory) — maps directly to branches. Use when assigning Perquisite or debating branch virtue.
