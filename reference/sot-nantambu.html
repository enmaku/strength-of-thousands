Overview

Nantambu is a canal-filled city-state on a bend of the Vanji River, at the western edge of the Mwangi Jungle. Glass charms tinkle from windows and doors; open-air markets smell of spice and rare wood; the Magaambya rises at the city’s heart on its own island — ten terraced towers ringed with mosaics of the Ten Magic Warriors.
  • Also called: Song-Wind City (Spoken on the Song Wind).
  • Founded: ~−3502 AR by Old-Mage Jatembe alongside the academy (timeline).
  • Trade hub: River traffic from the Inner Sea and jungle caravans meet here.
  • Population: ~14,500 (large city); mostly human Zenj, plus Bonuwat, Mauxi, Ekujae elves, kholo, halflings, iruxi, catfolk, and more.
  • At your table: Common = Mwangi (local language Xanmba also common). See Player's Guide.

Sources: Kindled Magic Campaign Overview; PathfinderWiki — Nantambu; The Mwangi Expanse (Lost Omens).

Government

Nantambu evolved from Jatembe’s seat of learning into a democratic haven. An elected council governs; citizens old enough vote and may run for office.

  • Magistrate-mayors: Eleven elected officials share executive duties. Magistrate-Mayor Asanda appears in Book 1 (Speaker’s Stage) and Book 2 (flooded manor plot).
  • Relationship to Magaambya: The academy enhances prestige and stability; conversants represent the school in the city. No formal army — protection flows from Magaambyan service.
  • Neighboring villages: 100+ settlements willingly pay tribute; they serve as eyes and ears in the jungle. Diplomatic treaties, not conquest.

Nantambiyan Tower

Tall landmark housing the Watcher’s Stone of Jatembe — an ancient scrying globe used to monitor threats such as Usaro. Useful for “someone is watching” foreshadowing.

Ten pillar landmarks

Magical monuments around the city — heavily warded. Chizire wants to enchant one to tell bawdy jokes (Book 1 student hook). Use as orientation anchors or conversant mission sites.

Soaring Spire (campus-adjacent)

Anti-gravity pillar at Spire Dorm — students hang-glide from the roof; original purpose lost to time.

Defense & threats

  • No walls, no standing army — Tempest-Sun Mages have never let a foe within 20 miles of the city.
  • Angazhan cultists and Aspis Consortium raiders historically give Nantambu wide berth.
  • Book 2 cracks: Asanda neglects office; Chime-Ringers stretched thin; Froglegs (grippli crime boss) exploits the gap; serpentfolk coup escalates.

Nantambu Chime-Ringers

Community-funded peacekeepers — not a standing army. Founded early in city history; traditionally coordinated with magistrate-mayors.

  • Book 2 strain: Asanda’s retreat → erratic protection → criminals emboldened.
  • Conversant rank: PCs may gain Nantambu Chime-Ringer archetype access.
  • Janatimo’s view: Chasing petty crime while Froglegs operates in the shadows — sends PCs instead.
