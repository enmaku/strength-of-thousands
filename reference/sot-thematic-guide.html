Campaign through-line

You stand on others' shoulders. Knowledge obligates you to serve beyond yourself. Becoming a guardian of tradition requires humility as much as courage — because the same curiosity that rebuilds civilization can destroy the community that nurtured it.

GM toolkit — encouraging engagement

Reuse these habits across the whole AP. They turn prefab beats into conversations your players remember.

Ask virtue questions, not lore quizzes. After major scenes: "Which branch virtue did that solution embody?" or "Which virtue did we fail to show?" Reward XP or Impression Points for thoughtful answers, not correct ones.
End sessions on reflection, not loot. Last 5 minutes: one player names something they learned about another PC, the school, or themselves. Tie to rank progression — initiates report to seniors; teachers debrief with students.
Keep a cohort journal (in-world). Uzunjati tradition: PCs maintain a shared log of service projects, theories, and names of people helped. Read entries aloud when Book 6 callbacks land (Xhokan's shop, Tahenkot's class, etc.).
Spirit mask check-ins. At First Masking and each rank ceremony, ask each PC: what animal did you choose and what part of yourself are you willing to show the community? Revisit at Magic Warrior renaming — what title replaces your old name?
Foreshadow Koride with admiration, not suspicion. Players should like Koride early. When the egg plot pays off, the tragedy is personal. Have her praise a PC's specimen work; argue with Esi about rules vs. discovery.
Let failures teach. Rain-Scribe ethos: wrong theories are valuable if you update them. When Janatimo's debate or an investigation goes sideways, celebrate the revision — "What do we believe now?"
Scale responsibility explicitly. When rank changes, narrate the new weight: conversants speak for the school in the city; lore-speakers shape young minds; Magic Warriors carry Jatembe's legacy. Ask: "What are you not ready for yet?"

Book 1 — Kindled Magic (levels 1→4) · Initiate → Attendant

Themes this book establishes

  • You inherit a tradition — welcome speech, Perquisite, Introduction Ceremony
  • Service before status — five branch tasks before anyone calls you attendant
  • Cohort belonging — shared dorm, shared rank, classmates with study bonuses
  • First identity ritual — spirit mask at First Masking
  • Institution has blind spots — Uduak expelled; gremlin history; insects never stop

Key NPC beats

Takulu Ot (interview): "Magic demands service" — have him ask each PC why they came, and whether they're ready to be ordinary on day one of the Perquisite.
Koride (Rain-Scribe task): Brilliant, dismissive of rules — players should want her approval. Note if a PC shares her love of "gross" discovery.
Esi vs. Koride: Rules-follower vs. rule-bender — let players pick sides without making either wrong.
Table habit to start here: Cohort journal + end-of-session "what did we learn about each other?"

Janatimo: Never give the egg secret away — validate every plausible theory. Model Tempest-Sun intellectual valor.
Asanda (before kidnapping): Not cartoonishly evil — busy, distant. Theme is neglect, not malice.

Finale engagement checklist

  1. Read one entry from the cohort journal from Book 1 or 2.
  2. Each PC reunites with one Spire Dorm friend during rescues.
  3. Koride's apology scene — let it breathe; no rush to combat.
  4. After Slana hatches: "You are no longer students. What do you teach now?"
