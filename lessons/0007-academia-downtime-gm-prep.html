Academia downtime — Study, Cram & Practical Research

How branch levels work, when to call for checks, and how the subsystem shifts from exams to field discovery across all six books.

Book 1 · NPC reference · AoN — Life in the Academy (share with players)

Baseline — Pathfinder 2e downtime

Before the Magaambya subsystem: downtime mode is how PF2e handles safe, low-stakes time between adventures — played day-by-day, usually summarized in a few minutes at session start or between chapters. One roll often stands in for a whole day’s work.

The Strength of Thousands AP adds Study, Cram, and Practical Research on top of this. PCs can still use standard activities whenever the story gives them free time — Lumusi even points initiates at Earn Income jobs in Book 1; Book 5’s teaching semester explicitly allows Crafting, Earn Income, and Learn a Spell alongside prep research.

ActivitySkillTypical use
Earn Income Crafting, Lore, Performance (+ others by negotiation) Job for pay; GM sets task level; roll once, then keep earning at that rate for days or weeks
Craft Crafting Make items from raw materials; days or weeks per project; needs formula for uncommon+ items
Learn a Spell Arcana, Nature, Occultism, or Religion Casters add spells to spellbook/repertoire; 1 hour per spell rank + material cost + skill check
Subsist Society or Survival Find food and shelter without paying; “subsistence” cost of living
Create Forgery Society Fabricate a document over a day or week
Treat Disease Medicine Long-term care toward curing an affliction
Retrain — Swap a feat (~1 week), skill increase (~1 week), or class feature (~1 month+); no other downtime while retraining

Also built into downtime mode: buying and selling gear, long-term rest (recover HP/conditions over days), and cost of living (comfortable ≈ 4 gp/month in a city). For weeks-to-years off, the GM picks a few standout events and averages the rest rather than rolling every day.

SoT vs default: Standard downtime is optional filler the players choose. Academia downtime is story-scheduled — the module tells you when to offer Study or Practical Research slots. Run both; they answer different questions (pocket money vs branch level).

The one-sentence pitch

Branch levels (primary + secondary) advance through semester-scale downtime checks, not XP — Study and Cram while students, then Practical Research in the field once they graduate to teachers — and the real payoff is a parallel track of lore, skill feats, and Magaambyan Attendant archetype feats tied to each branch.

Sources: Archives of Nethys — Life in the Academy, Kindled Magic pp. 61–63, per-book Practical Research sidebars.

Core concept — two level tracks

  • Each PC picks a primary branch and secondary branch (both start at level 0).
  • Primary branch level cannot exceed character level.
  • Secondary branch level cannot exceed half character level (minimum 0).
  • Branch levels grant benefits at specific thresholds (lore, skill feats, branch feats, Magaambyan Attendant dedication at branch 3, etc.) — see Table 2 in the rules article.
  • Each downtime activity represents roughly a semester (a few months). Adventures happen during or between semesters as the story requires.
Share with players: The AoN “Life in the Academy” article is written from the player perspective and is not a secret. Hand it out at session zero so they understand branch choice and advancement before you run the first Study check.

The star rule (banking progress)

If a check would raise a branch past its cap, mark a ★ next to that branch level. When the PC next gains a character level, bump the branch to the new cap and erase the star. Players never waste a good roll.

Three downtime activities

Study

Requirements: initiate, attendant, or conversant.

Pick primary or secondary branch. Roll one of that branch’s associated skills against the standard DC for the branch’s current level.

ResultEffect
Critical success+2 branch levels
Success+1 branch level
FailureNo change
Critical failureSkip the next Study opportunity (remedial work)

AoN — Study

Cram

Requirements: initiate, attendant, or conversant; primary or secondary branch is 2+ levels below its cap.

Perform Study twice (each branch must qualify). Until the PC takes Study again normally, each adventuring day starts with a DC 8 flat check or they are fatigued that day.

GM tip: Cram is a catch-up lever, not the default rhythm. Book 1 explicitly suggests discouraging it early — fatigue during insect fights and masking ceremony hurts.

AoN — Cram

Practical Research

Requirements: conversant or lore-speaker.

Field discovery — the GM describes an appropriate check when the opportunity arises. Results usually mirror Study, but some hooks grant unique options (new spells, access to locations, etc.).

Post-graduation rule (Book 3 sidebar): Lore-speakers teach, but they do not advance branches by teaching alone. Field discovery — adding scholarship, recovering lost knowledge — is how teachers progress. Book 5 Ch 1 reinforces this: the semester of teaching does not increase branch levels; the Akiton expedition is the Practical Research lever.

Spire Dorm study bonuses

When a PC reaches Liked (3+ hearts) with a Spire student on the relationship tracker, that student’s Classroom Advantage applies to Study/Cram for their branch — e.g. Anchor Root turns Rain-Scribe crit fails into fails; Esi upgrades Tempest-Sun successes to crit successes.

Weave dorm friendships into semester scenes; the mechanical bonus rewards the fiction you’re already playing. Full text lives in the app’s Relationships page and NPC reference.

Running it at the table

Roleplay before you roll

Paizo’s guidance: the subsystem shines when Study is a scene — hunting a missing library text, convincing a professor to approve a risky experiment, dorm study group with an NPC who grants Classroom Advantage. Roll after the fiction, not instead of it.

Pacing anti-patterns

  • Calendar paralysis: You don’t owe four Study checks before the next combat. Book 1 says 2–4 — pick what fits your table.
  • Semester monotony (Book 5): If players only roll research checks, run faculty events and remind them of Crafting, Earn Income, Learn Spell, etc.
  • Forgetting rank gates: Study/Cram require initiate through conversant. Lore-speakers need Practical Research hooks.
  • Ignoring crit fail skip: Track who owes remedial time — it’s a story hook (tutor sessions, embarrassed PC).

If your table wants less paperwork

AoN offers an official shortcut: skip the subsystem entirely (PCs are slightly less versatile), or auto-advance primary branch at every even character level to a max of 11 at level 20. Use this only if players opted out at session zero — the AP assumes branch growth.
