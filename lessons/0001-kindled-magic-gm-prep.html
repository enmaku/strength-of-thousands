Kindled Magic — what you need before session one

Plot threads, NPCs, chapter beats, and foreshadowing. Read once, run confidently.

The one-sentence pitch

New initiates at the Magaambya — Golarion's oldest magic academy — complete their Perquisite (mandatory public service), earn their spirit masks, and uncover that campus insect swarms and gremlin sabotage trace back to a vengeful spirit in the walls… while a secret artifact in a professor's lab is the real long-term threat the whole AP builds toward.

GM-only: the hidden main villain line
  • Vesicant Egg — indestructible artifact; fragments of the King of Biting Ants. Rain-Scribe entomologist Koride Ulawa acquired it and hid it on campus. It draws insects and pests toward the Magaambya (and may have drawn the anadi students in Book 1's climax). Not revealed to players in Book 1.
  • Serpentfolk — spies seek the egg's power (major in Book 2+). Koride's secret research and shadow-plane activity attract them.
  • Jatembe's enemies — AP framing: Old-Mage Jatembe vanished; enemies still plot against his school. Pays off late.

Setting essentials

Magaambya & Nantambu

  • Academy in the heart of Nantambu, Song-Wind City on the Vanji River (Mwangi Expanse).
  • Founded ~−2832 AR by disciples of Old-Mage Jatembe and the Ten Magic Warriors (animal-mask legends).
  • Philosophy: "Word and Way" — study and apply knowledge in service to others. Isolationist perpetual study is looked down on.
  • Head: High Sun-Mage Oyamba (Tempest-Sun). Most faculty are abroad at any given time; campus still feels lived-in via students & staff.

Rank progression (whole AP)

Initiate → complete Perquisite (public service) → Attendant (wooden spirit mask) → Conversant (city reps) → Lore-speaker (teacher). Book 1 covers initiate through attendant; pace is fast by design.

Player's Guide

Book 1 structure — three chapters

Levels 1→4. Calendar starts Arodus 4721 (rainy season). Combat exists but mystery, relationships, and clever solutions are the main rewards — not hack-and-slash.

Chapter 1 — beat by beat

Opening

  • PCs arrive as a cohort (not a random adventuring party — shared dorm, shared rank).
  • Takulu Ot (Cascade Bearer teacher) conducts the group interview — often at Welcome Walk or Tireless Hall. Friendly diplomat; mbira player; loves anteaters. Becomes recurring mentor.
  • Welcome speech: magic demands service. PCs get brief campus exploration, then the Perquisite.

Perquisite — five service projects (5 days + ceremony)

Spire Dorm seniors assign one task per day (Esi organized them by branch). Day 6 = meet teachers; then a rest day and classes. Order in book:

  1. Rain-Scribes — Ojofiri chicks from Kafesi Market (Magaambya boycotts market; PCs pass as new). Esi + Haibram.
  2. Tempest-Sun — Remove pugwampi gremlins from storage barn non-lethally. Esi mentions expelled student Uduak. Secret hatch → tunnels.
  3. Cascade Bearers — Gather magical components (Chizire/Mariama assignment).
  4. Emerald Boughs — Ritual / outreach task (Ignaci/Tzeniwe).
  5. Uzunjati — Mail delivery into Nantambu + recount stories (Okoro/Noxolo).

Day 6: Introduction Ceremony at Tireless Hall — gremlin saboteurs attack. PCs reach level 2 after defeating them.

Chapter 1 antagonists (first hints)

  • Kurshkin — pugwampi; unluck aura; tied to gremlin attack on ceremony.
  • Stone Ghost — not named yet; gremlins use tunnels beneath campus.
  • Library research on Uduak / pugwampis rewards curious PCs early.

Chapter 2 — the school year

Academia subsystem (your pacing lever)

  • Between scenes: PCs Study primary/secondary branch → ranks → bonus feats/skills (Magaambyan Attendant archetype at rank 3).
  • Book suggests 2–4 Study opportunities this chapter. Pair each with a "meet a teacher" or campus event.
  • Cramming = study both branches at once but stay fatigued — discourage early.
  • Typical GM calendar: ~4 Study checks + 4 events → masking ceremony ~1 year after arrival.

First Masking (emotional peak)

  • Held in Tireless Hall. Each PC crafts a wooden spirit mask (animal face = spirit face / Magic Warrior tradition). Initiates → attendants.
  • Fellow students' masks are described in "Students of the Magaambya" — use for color (Esi's buffalo, Tzeniwe's peacock spider with crooked eyes from her kids, etc.).
  • Ceremony interrupted by a major insect attack — giant bees, silverfish swarms, tsetse flies. PCs reach level 3 after.

Other chapter 2 beats (interleave with Study)

  • Fetch quests, a joust/tournament (Mafika's mecha-leshies), rituals, Koride's entomology lectures (repulsive but charismatic).
  • Tzeniwe's surprise party — slice-of-life with her spiderling twins.
  • Relationship track: students move from Liked → Helpful (grants study bonuses). Not everyone will love every PC — that's fine.

Chapter epigraphs

Each chapter opens with a fable or in-world vignette. Author intent (Ron Lundeen, Paizo forums): mood and theme, not player spoilers. Read aloud or paraphrase.

  • Ch 1 — Jatembe fable: bad-luck spirit targets his "children" (students).
  • Ch 2 — Mountain woman: magic-users need respect or they withdraw.
  • Ch 3 — Bee dance: trickster teaches communication (foreshadows investigation & teamwork).

Chapter 3 — the truth in the tunnels

Investigation

After the insect surge, PCs (and faculty) need answers. Trail leads to tunnels / abandoned sections beneath campus. Hazards include trapped centipede carcasses, gremlin nests, and **magical mask treasures** (Ot can transfer powers to PCs' own masks).

Stone Ghost — Book 1 BBEG

Uduak Basni — former oread student, cruel and selfish. During his Perquisite he massacred gremlins instead of removing them peacefully (the sin Esi warns against). Expelled; attacked teachers; Magaambya wards rebounded and seemingly disintegrated him.

He survived merged with the stone walls. Wards hide him. He commands gremlins, takes the name Stone Ghost, digs tunnels, and plots revenge. Body: stone chunks, mosaic face, shattered ward-runes.

Module may involve kidnapped students — some GMs soften this (students endangered but not murdered) to preserve trust in the academy.

Kurshkin — secondary villain

Immortal pugwampi; grudge against Takulu Ot (he stuffed her in a sack on an elephant ~4698). Her gremlin friends were killed by Uduak. Returns ~4719, allying with Stone Ghost. Fightable non-lethally; may negotiate truce after Book 1 if handled with care.

Anadi arrival & griffons

  • Anadi forms matter: three shapes (human, spider, hybrid). Tzeniwe usually presents human; her portrait in “Students of the Magaambya” is human-form — correct. Twins shift freely (spiderling on her back, human child on knee).
  • New arrivals Goss, Mazta, Savana appear in hybrid form at the Speaker’s Stage — atypical for travelers; Society DC 15 = they’re new to Nantambu customs. Some classmates are unsettled (Esi is fine — friends with Tzeniwe).
  • Griffons attack them (unusual predation). PCs intervene; faculty backup for climax.
  • GM layer: egg's pull may explain both anadi and insects — don't reveal yet; Janatimo might note the coincidence later.

Book 1 ending

Defeat or banish Stone Ghost. Campus safer. PCs are level 4 attendants with masks. Insects don't fully stop — Koride's egg still beckons. Bridge to Book 2: conversant duties in Nantambu, serpentfolk interest.

GM tips — prefab-friendly

  • Read Campaign Overview + Ch 1 before session 1; skim Ch 2–3 and "Students" / "Life in the Academy" before month-two play.
  • Mwangi Expanse book — optional for Book 1 (mostly campus); valuable for mail run and essential later.
  • Hand out all service projects at once if the fetch chain feels railroady; fellow students advise, not command.
  • Reward creativity — XP for service, mysteries, and friends, not just combat (Paizo sidebar).
  • Common = Mwangi in this AP, not Taldane.
  • Ch 3 content warning — animal cruelty; check table consent.
  • Insects are the AP's through-line enemy type; Lean into creepy-crawlies.
  • Everyone should feel magical — Player's Guide gives dabblers cantrips; casters get more. Non-casters aren't second-class.
  • Prefab handles maps/stats — your job is pacing, NPC voice, and connecting beats to branch virtues.

What to foreshadow in Book 1 (don't explain yet)

  • Insects keep coming back no matter what you clear.
  • Koride is weirdly interested in specimens; she plays favorites (Anchor Root) and antagonizes Esi.
  • Gremlin history on campus is older and bloodier than current pranks suggest.
  • Anadi arrival feels fated — Janatimo might call it a blessing in disguise.
  • Faculty trust PCs with real responsibility absurdly fast — lean into "legendary cohort" tone or add slice-of-life to slow burn.
