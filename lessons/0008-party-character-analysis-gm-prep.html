Anya

Versatile Human · Summoner (Beast eidolon) · Feral Child · Cha-key · Fleet (Speed 30)

Class features that matter

  • Beast eidolon — a primal magical-beast manifestation (claws/jaws style melee). Unlike Yggostrus's dragon, this one is primal tradition and built for closing the gap.
  • Beast's Charge two-actions — eidolon Strides twice then Strikes (+1 if it moved 20+ ft in a straight line). Great for opening on gremlins or repositioning in the barn/tunnels. (AoN: Beast's Charge)
  • Glider Form (evolution feat) — the eidolon can glide; session-zero flavor has it watching rooftops like perches and gliding between trees.
  • Act Together / Share Senses / Link Spells — same two-body action economy as Yggostrus. Focus: Boost Eidolon, Evolution Surge.

Ancestry, skills & background

  • Feral Child + Forager + trained Survival / Athletics / Nature — the party's wilderness toolkit. Magaambya campus greens, foraging, living rough: hand Anya these beats.
  • Fleet (from Versatile Human) → Speed 30. Natural Skill is on the sheet; one of its skill picks still shows as Not Selected in PathBuilder — confirm with the player.
  • Intimidation + Deception (Cha 16) — a rough, wild-side face, complementary to Yggostrus's Diplomacy. Low-light vision; eidolon also has imprecise scent.
  • Spontaneous primal summoner casting — but no spells are entered yet in the hero file. Don't assume cantrips until the player fills them in.
Protect both summoners. Anya is Medium (not Tiny) but still unarmored AC 14 — the party's lowest — and shares HP with the eidolon. Same failure mode as Yggostrus: drop the summoner, lose two bodies. With two summoners at the table, screen both back-liners or accept that a focused gremlin swarm can delete half the party's combat presence.

Book 1 hooks

Session zero: she grew up in the wild with the beast bond and came to the Magaambya to learn what the bond means and how to live among people. That's a clean hook into cohort friendship (dorm life as culture-shock), Rain-Scribe outdoor work, and the school's "serve the community" ethos. Play her as the student still learning classrooms, manners, and markets — Cy’An's cooking and Taraan's social toolkit become mentoring moments.

contrast

Two summoners, two vibes: Yggostrus is an arcane dragonet marvel faculty will gawk at; Anya is a feral primal bond that looks more like Magaambya's own nature-magic tradition. Faculty reactions can split — fascination vs. "will she adapt?" — without crowding the same beat.

Cy’An Copperbottom

Irongut Goblin · Inventor (Construct Innovation) · Wizard Dedication (School of Unified Magical Theory) · Cook · Int-key · they/them

Class features that matter

  • Construct companion — a built minion that acts on a Command. A second body on the map for the Perquisite, the gremlin fights, and the tunnels. Mechanically a "golem" Cy’An made — thematically right at home at the Magaambya.
  • Overdrive (Crafting check) → bonus damage on Strikes. Their turn-one ramp.
  • Explode lethal AoE — 2d6 fire, basic Reflex, 5-ft emanation. This is the party's only non-spellcaster area attack and a genuine swarm answer. (AoN: Explode)
  • Wizard Dedication ties them directly to the school's arcane curriculum. Prepared arcane: Prestidigitation (the ultimate utility/flavor cantrip) and Eat Fire.

Spell to know its real text

Eat Fire is not a flamethrower. It's a reaction when they'd take fire damage: fire resistance 5, plus a one-action Belch Smoke that makes a concealing cloud. So it's a defensive/utility trick (handy if Haibram's pyromania goes sideways, or to break line of sight), not an attack. (AoN: Eat Fire)

Feats

  • City Scavenger — Subsist/Earn Income bonuses in a city; great for the Nantambu errands and the Kafesi Market boycott.
  • Built-In Tools, Shield Block (note: no shield in their kit — they'd need to grab one to use it), Seasoned (cooking/alchemy crafting).
  • Irongut Goblin heritage — eats spoiled/dangerous food safely. Pure gold for a Cook at a market the school boycotts.
  • Darkvision — useful in Ch 3.

Book 1 hooks

Their Cook background + Cooking Lore + the Bonding Meal ritual are a direct line to Lumusi Yao (dining hall) and the cohort-friendship theme — let Cy’An cook dorm meals and the table's relationships write themselves. The Irongut goblin who'll eat anything is the obvious pick for the Kafesi Market run.

Tension

Their most efficient tools (Explode, a construct that bites) are emphatically lethal. The Perquisite explicitly asks for non-lethal gremlin removal, and the whole Stone Ghost arc is about a student who slaughtered gremlins. A goblin gleefully exploding pests is exactly the temptation the module is built around — surface it, don't punish it.

Solanum Calabash

Fruit Leshy · Magus (Twisting Tree) · Astrologer · Str-key · she/her

Class features that matter

  • Spellstrike + Arcane Cascade — channels a spell through a melee hit, then stays in a stance for bonus damage. Str 18 + a d8 bo staff (+7 to hit) makes Solanum a real front-liner, not a glass caster.
  • Twisting Tree hybrid study → staff specialist; Spinning Staff conflux focus spell sweeps adjacent foes. Note: these are physical Strikes — resisted by swarms, so point Solanum at the gremlins/oread, not the bug clouds.
  • Healing Plaster cantrip — out-of-combat healing on tap, backing up Taraan's font. (prepared arcane caster, Int-based)

Spells & ancestry

  • Bramble Bush / Tangle Vine / Take Root — battlefield control and difficult terrain; useful for funneling gremlins or capturing them alive.
  • Befuddle — a non-damaging way to neutralize a target (helps the non-lethal mandate).
  • Seedpod (Fruit Leshy) — a thrown ranged option with a dazzle rider. As a Fruit Leshy she can also offer a once-daily fruit an ally eats to heal — a third healing source in the party.
  • As a leshy she doesn't sleep/breathe like the others — handy in the tunnels and for night-watch flavor.

Book 1 hooks

Occultism + Oddity Identification make Solanum your go-to for the campus's uncanny mystery — identifying the haunt-like Stone Ghost and odd magical phenomena. The Astrologer background + Astrology Lore is a quiet long-game gift: let Solanum read omens in the sky. foreshadow The campaign's Book 5 is literally Doorway to the Red Star — drop celestial portents now and they'll feel earned later.

Taraan Skyseeker

Skyborn Tengu · Cleric (Warpriest of Soralyon) / Witch (The Inscribed One) — dual-class · Archaeologist · Wis + Int

Class features that matter

  • Healing Font — four extra Heal spells/day on top of prepared slots. Taraan is the primary healer; the academic-attrition pacing of Book 1 is very survivable with him in the cohort.
  • Dual-class Cleric/Witch — two full spell lists (divine and arcane) plus warpriest armor/weapon training. Enormous flexibility: he can prep for the day's expected problem.
  • Familiar — Elemental Wisp (Earth) with Burrower, Speech, Accompanist. A talking, burrowing scout is tailor-made for the Ch 3 tunnels. Phase Familiar focus spell keeps it alive; Discern Secrets focus cantrip aids investigation.

Skills, lore & gear

  • Trained in almost the entire skill list with Int 18 / Wis 16 — the party's academia downtime and mystery backbone.
  • Archaeologist + Ancient Regional Lore + Additional Lore — ruins, tunnels, and the shattered ward-runes on the Stone Ghost are squarely his wheelhouse.
  • Ring of Discretion (hide a magic aura) and Glasses of Sociability (impression bonus) make him a sneaky-good social/utility piece. Wordreaper is a +1 weapon. He's also cash-rich (~300 gp) if you want a shopping beat at Xhokan's.
GM gold — Soralyon is the perfect god for this book.

Soralyon, the Mystic Angel, is the empyreal lord of guardians, monuments, and magic: study and protect ancient sites, craft golems and artwork from stone, never defile sacred buildings or destroy historical artifacts. His symbol is a rune-covered spire. (AoN: Soralyon)

  • The Magaambya itself — the oldest magic academy, an ancient institution worth guarding — is a Soralyon cause. His faith reads as a divine mandate to defend the school.
  • The Book 1 villain is an oread (earth) ex-student fused into the stone walls, covered in broken ward-runes. A Soralyon cleric facing a desecrated, runed stone revenant under the campus is thematically electric — and his anathema (don't defile sacred buildings / destroy artifacts) gives Taraan a real stake in how the party resolves it.
  • The Inscribed One witch patron (the written word, secrets) marries cleanly to the Magaambya's Word and Way and the Uzunjati branch. Soralyon's rune-spire iconography even rhymes with it.
  • Let Taraan be the moral center of the Stone Ghost finale — the voice asking whether Uduak gets destroyed or laid to rest, and how the institution failed him.
  • campaign Longer witch/patron arc (Ird, Erudite, Jatembe) lives in Player Tie-Ins — don't spill it in Book 1.

Yggostrus

Dragonet (Homing Drake) · Summoner (Dragon eidolon, acid) · Eidolon Contact · Apsu · Cha-key · he/him

Class features that matter

  • Dragon eidolon — the front-line bruiser (teeth, claws). Yggostrus the tiny dragonet stays back and directs it.
  • Acid Breath Weapon + Energy Heart (acid) area — a cone of acid is the single best swarm-clearer in the party.
  • Act Together / Share Senses / Link Spells — two-body action economy and a built-in scout (see through the eidolon).
  • Boost Eidolon and Evolution Surge focus spells pump the eidolon mid-fight.

Spells & skills

  • Spontaneous arcane (Cha 18): Caustic Blast area (5-ft burst of acid — triggers swarm weakness), Daze, Protect Companion, Timber, Root Reading; Weave Wood, Flourishing Flora. Innate Homing Drake cantrip: Know the Way. (AoN: Caustic Blast)
  • Diplomacy + Intimidation with Cha 18 — the party's polished Diplomacy face (Anya covers the rough Intimidation/Deception lane). Dubious Knowledge means his Recall Knowledge always returns one truth and one plausible lie: lean into it for comedy at the lore-heavy academy.
  • Acid resistance (Dragonet Resistances); darkvision; languages Common + Draconic. Deity Apsu — a dragon-god faith that rhymes with both his ancestry and the Magaambya's ancient magic-warrior lineage if you want a quiet religious beat.
Protect the summoner. Yggostrus is Tiny, unarmored, AC 15, and shares his HP with the eidolon — if he goes down, the eidolon disappears and the party loses two combatants at once. Same risk as Anya. Gremlins and the Stone Ghost focusing a summoner is a real party-wiper vector. Watch for it, and let allies/the eidolon screen him.

Party-wide tactical read

Swarms & insects — you're well-armed, but only on the right axis

Book 1 throws swarms and giant insects at the party repeatedly (the masking-ceremony attack, silverfish, Ch 3 centipedes). Swarms are resistant to physical damage, immune to precision, and have a weakness to area & splash damage. (AoN: Swarm trait)

  • Real anti-swarm tools: Yggostrus (Caustic Blast + acid breath) is your MVP; Cy’An's Explode is the backup. Both are area damage that hits the swarm weakness.
  • Resisted against swarms: Solanum's staff Spellstrikes, both eidolons' melee, Cy’An's construct, Taraan's Needle Darts (single-target). Don't let melee players feel useless — give them the gremlins, the oread, and the giant single bugs while the AoE crew handles clouds.

The non-lethal mandate vs. this party's toolkit

The Perquisite (and the Stone Ghost's whole backstory) hinge on removing gremlins without slaughtering them. This party's signature moves — acid breath, Explode, two sets of eidolon teeth — are decidedly lethal. That's a feature: the temptation to nuke pests is the exact mirror of Uduak's sin. Give them clean non-lethal outs and reward using them:

  • Solanum's bo staff (bludgeoning, easy to declare non-lethal) and Befuddle / Tangle Vine.
  • Taraan's Charm / Forbidding Ward and sheer skill toolkit (talk them out, trick them).
  • Cy’An's goblin ingenuity — sacks, traps, bait (they're a cook; lure gremlins with food).
  • Yggostrus's Diplomacy / Anya's Intimidation — negotiate or scare them off instead of killing.

Healing & resilience

The party is healing-rich: Taraan's Healing Font (4 Heals/day) + Solanum's Healing Plaster cantrip + the Fruit Leshy fruit. You can run the attrition-style academic year without coddling them. Fragile members: Taraan (14 HP), Anya (AC 14, shared eidolon HP), and Yggostrus (Tiny, AC 15, shared eidolon HP) — all three want screening. Two summoners means two separate "summoner drops → eidolon vanishes" clocks.

Table prep & open questions

  • Branch picks aren't in PathBuilder. Confirm each hero's primary/secondary branch at session zero. Natural fits to offer (not impose): Cy’An → Cascade Bearers (invention) or Rain-Scribes; Solanum → Rain-Scribes (adaptability) or Tempest-Sun (martial); Taraan → Uzunjati (knowledge / Word and Way); Yggostrus → Emerald Boughs (camaraderie) or Tempest-Sun; Anya → Rain-Scribes (wild craft) or Emerald Boughs (belonging).
  • Anya's PathBuilder sheet is incomplete — empty spell list, one Natural Skill still "Not Selected," no armor/weapons entered. Get those before scenes that assume her casting.
  • Shield Block on Cy’An does nothing until they carry a shield — flag it if they want the reaction online.
  • Decide your Stone Ghost ending early — Taraan (Soralyon) gives you a built-in advocate for a redemption/laying-to-rest beat rather than a kill. Know which way you're steering before Ch 3.
  • Spotlight balance: Taraan can solo a lot of investigation/social scenes. Deliberately hand non-Taraan players the Kafesi run (Cy’An / Anya Forager), the uncanny-ID checks (Solanum), Diplomacy (Yggostrus), and wilderness/forage (Anya).
  • Two-summoner action economy — the map gets crowded (Anya + beast, Yggostrus + dragon, Cy’An + construct). Keep gremlin fights readable; don't also stack extra minions on top without a reason.
