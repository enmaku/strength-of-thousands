The one-sentence pitch

PCs return from Akiton with **Old-Mage Jatembe**, become **Magic Warriors** — then discover **Koride** and their original cohort enslaved by the hatching **Vesicant Egg**. After a **Honey-I-Shrunk** fable on Koride’s lab table, a **colossal larva** wrecks campus; the finale is disaster-movie rescues, a campus-wide binding ritual at **the Circle**, and a three-boss fight against the **King of Biting Ants’s** split psyche — ending with a benevolent **Vesicant Guardian** ant.

Prerequisite: Assumes Book 5 (*Doorway to the Red Star*) — PCs rescued Jatembe from the King aboard the *Mandibles of Fate*. The King’s defeat triggered his contingency: the egg awakens.

What this book pays off (Book 1 → now)

  • Vesicant Egg — Koride was supposed to destroy it; she kept it at the **Protea Pavilion** instead. Acid from a fracture eats shadows → victims become **Unshadowed** drones.
  • Insect swarms — since Kindled Magic; the egg was always calling vermin.
  • Original cohort — Esi, Haibram, Ignaci, Okoro, etc. reappear as Unshadowed helpers; Koride absent from Jatembe’s ceremony.
  • Magic Warrior masks — Jatembe gives PCs new **Magic Warrior** titles (renaming ritual — the world remembers only their title, not their old names).
  • Dignitaries — Asanda, Father Heveril, Umanja-jinga of Osibu, Warder-Chief Mpondo (Iobane) attend; influence luncheon with Oyamba.

Chapter 1 — Unshadowed Academy

Opening celebration (don’t rush this)

  • Massive ceremony at the name obelisk; Jatembe names PCs **Magic Warriors** (first living holders in millennia).
  • PCs demonstrate prowess; **renaming ritual** — everyone except Jatembe & other PCs remembers them only by chosen Magic Warrior title.
  • Koride is absent (DC 35 Perception to notice). So is the rest of the PCs’ original cohort.
  • Cameos: Esi (skeptical teacher), Tzeniwe + grown twins Zachva & Zanvi.

Luncheon — influence subsystem

Four rounds with dignitaries (Gamemastery Guide p.151). Impress Asanda, Heveril, Umanja-jinga, Mpondo for political rewards. Beaded bracelet luck charms optional.

Investigation → Protea Pavilion

  • Cohort members (Ignaci, Okoro, etc.) act oddly — running Koride’s insect research.
  • Pavilion **relocated/hidden** in overgrown hedges; thorn guardians, **Tatterthread** (First World creature).
  • Unshadowed Koride — egg’s acid ate her shadow; true Koride still fights inside.
  • Defeating/curing her triggers egg fracture → PCs **shrunk** onto the Endless Table.

Chapter 2 — The Endless Table

Deliberately **fable/fairy-tale** tone — whimsical problems, improbable NPCs. Not a combat grind; exploration and story logic.

  • PCs smaller than ants; ordinary lab objects are dungeons.
  • Red ant queen wants to marry the returning King; ant society blocks the party.
  • Roamers: **Adanyini** the kitten (Anchor Root → Haibram), clockwork hazards.
  • Collect 4 shadow fragments; earn **Hlakana** the norn’s favor → restore Koride, remove Unshadowed.
  • Practical Research downtime on the table (branch levels up to 20!).
  • Claiming the **Vesicant Egg** restores PC size — but hatching continues.

Chapter 3 — The Unborn King

Larva as disaster (key GM note)

The larva is **too big to fight with normal damage**. Treat it like an earthquake + acid flood. PCs save people first:

  • Tahenkot’s Tempest-Sun class — trapped on acid island (slowed by psychic birth cry).
  • Osirian diplomats — Izem Mezitani sheltering them (romance subplot optional).
  • Xhokan’s Powderpile — supply store endangered.

Split party OK; time pressure; acid splash escalates if rescues drag.

The Circle ritual

  • Koride (contrite, shadow restored) proposes containing the larva; Jatembe leads combined ritual at campus edge.
  • Allies coordinate at **Ten Magic Warrior statues**: Oyamba, Takulu, Ahassunu, Esi, Noxolo, etc.
  • PCs **guard Jatembe** at the Circle for ~1 hour — waves of wereants & ant swarms; DC 35 Arcana/Nature each round or extra monsters spawn.

Mind of the King (final combat)

Net forces larva to burst; three psychic aspects manifest:

AspectRepresentsKey weakness (Evaluate Aspect DC 40)
Aspect of HungerMindless consumption (crimson worm)Divine & primal spells
Aspect of InsectsInsectile nature (multi-headed mukradi-like)Arcane & occult spells
Aspect of ImmortalityVictory over death (grim reaper skeleton)Arcane & primal spells

Destroying one aspect heals others +100 HP — kill order matters. Aspects don’t flank together.

Epilogue — Vesicant Guardian

After all three fall, larva dissolves. A **giant black ant** (Slana / Vesicant Guardian) hatches — Jatembe’s intended guardian, corrupted long ago, now freed. She becomes the Magaambya’s new defender. Campaign ends.

GM tips

  • Let Book 1 callbacks land — name every Spire Dorm friend you can in Ch 1 & 3 rescues.
  • Ch 2 tone shift — play it lighter; contrast makes Ch 3 devastation hit harder.
  • Ch 3 is frantic — short rests scarce; familiar faces need saving, not just strangers.
  • Renaming ritual — confirm with players OOC; their old names vanish from NPC memories (except party + Jatembe).
  • Campaign Repercussions (book p.75) — optional hooks: Jatembe’s travels, Walkena’s revenge, Osibu’s Nemesis Well, “one ant survived on Akiton…”
