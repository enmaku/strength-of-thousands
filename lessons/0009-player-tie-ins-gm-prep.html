How to use this doc

Each entry is a GM-owned truth plus drip-feed beats and named Magaambya relationships. These plots should never steal the AP’s spotlight — they flavor downtime, faculty asides, dreams, dorm friendship, and late conversations with figures the AP already puts on stage.

  • Foreshadow lightly in Books 1–4; pay off when the AP already hands you emotional room (Book 5 rescue / Book 6 Magic Warrior aftermath / Unshadowed crisis).
  • If a player invents a conflicting backstory at the table, prefer their version and revise here.
  • Only Taraan gets a private Jatembe confession beat. Everyone else pays off on a different stage.
  • Cross-link: Party analysis · Campus NPCs · Jatembe history · Book 5.

Taraan Skyseeker — The living city of Ird

Skyborn Tengu · Cleric of Soralyon / Witch (The Inscribed One) · Archaeologist · familiar: Erudite (“Eru”) · he/him
Dorm: Okoro · Faculty: Izem Mezitani (Janatimo secondary)

Relationships

  • Okoro Obiyo — fellow Uzunjati social glue; game night and stories. Lets Taraan be moral/lore center without only hanging with ghosts.
  • Izem Mezitani — winged aasimar archaeologist; dig sites, monument ethics, “never defile the find.” Stone Ghost prep without spoilers.
  • Janatimo (Book 2+) — cohort mentor for Word and Way; keep lighter than Izem so the dig-site bond stays personal.
  • Ignaci (light) — secrets / coded notes; archaeologist catnip. Don’t out-spook Noxolo/Lithrei.

The truth GM only

Ird was never a city of stone that happened to be ruled by monsters. Ird is a sapient magical entity — a living library-city, a knowledge-being whose body is architecture, glyph, and memory. The immortal ruling caste of legend were parasites: they bound Ird, harvested its power, and trafficked in the souls of those who lived within its streets.

Old-Mage Jatembe and the Ten Magic Warriors defeated those masters and freed Ird. Jatembe could not bring himself to destroy a living, thinking thing that had been enslaved. He also could not leave it findable. So he helped Ird hide itself with powerful magics and seeded the public story that he destroyed the city — a false trail meant to keep every treasure-hunter, soul-trader, and would-be master from ever looking in the right place.

That living city is Taraan’s witch patron: the force he knows as The Inscribed One. When they met, no words were spoken — glyphs moved across a presence that was street and scripture at once. The meeting was sealed behind amnesia so that Taraan cannot lead anyone (including himself, consciously) back to Ird. Erudite is a fragment of that contact: a burrowing earth wisp written over in an unknown script that shifts when hexes flow.

One-line pitch

Jatembe freed a living city and lied that he destroyed it; that city is Taraan’s patron, and neither of them is allowed to say so — until, perhaps, he is strong enough to remember and still keep the secret.

What the world believes published lore

  • Ird’s location is unknown; the subject is taboo. Native guides mislead seekers and abandon them in the jungle.
  • Surviving artifacts paint a culture of slavery, decadence, and immortal nobles who traded in lives and souls.
  • Some credit Jatembe with destroying Ird; others (loudly censured at the Magaambya) claim it was his birthplace.
  • Some say Ird originated the magic that created the Harvestmen (Umasi).
  • No firsthand accounts; knowledge filters through later cultures and Nantambu’s sages.

Your truth explains all of that. It does not contradict a confirmed ruin map — there isn’t one.

The Inscribed One = Ird

Patron text already fits: silent meeting; words and glyphs instead of speech; possible archmage or abandoned artifact seeking a successor. Treat Ird as the latter taken literally — not a dead object, but a liberated city-mind looking for a careful steward, not a new master.

The moral fracture (play this)

Taraan can cheer the destruction of tyrants and still be furious at the idea of razing a city. As a Soralyon cleric and archaeologist, destroying the site itself is the wound. Early on, that reads as scholarly distaste for a Magaambya-approved legend. Late, when the truth lands, it becomes: Jatembe did the right thing and then lied about it — and the lie is what Taraan’s faith would have demanded all along.

That is the contention, not “was Jatembe evil?” Frame the argument as preservation vs. the story told to protect the preserved thing.

Amnesia rules table handling

  • Taraan may know he has a patron and that the meeting is blank. Dreams, glyphs, and Eru’s behavior can leak mood without coordinates.
  • Recall Knowledge about Ird can return the public legend. Critical successes might feel wrong in his gut (“destroyed” sticks in his throat) without explaining why.
  • Do not let divination casually pinpoint Ird. Magaambya faculty shut down Ird talk; treat that as both culture and residual ward.
  • Erudite may refuse to write certain names, or rewrite “Ird” into shifting nonsense, if the player pushes too hard too early.

The Jatembe conversation target scene

Ideal timing: quiet moment after the Akiton rescue (Book 5) or after the Magic Warrior ceremony (Book 6), when Jatembe is present but deferring spotlight. Taraan presses the destruction story. Jatembe does not confess in open court.

“As your power grows, you may be allowed to remember.”

“But…” — a sly wink — “you will still not be allowed to tell.” Old-Mage Jatembe → Taraan

Implication: Jatembe recognizes the patron’s mark. He is not the patron. He is the city’s co-conspirator in the lie. He can only help unveil what Ird itself is ready to return.

Do not make this the campaign climax. The King of Biting Ants / Vesicant Egg remain the AP spine. Ird is a personal chord — a reason Taraan’s witch half matters when Jatembe is finally on stage.

Optional complications (pick later)

  • Pathfinder / Aspis hunters still search for Ird. A rumor that a Magaambya student “smells of Ird” could draw heat without revealing the truth.
  • A surviving master’s echo — not the patron, but a hungry remnant that wants the city bound again.
  • Harvestmen lore — Ird-as-origin of Umasi grafting magic was the masters’ craft, not the city’s will.

Open questions

  • Does Taraan ever visit Ird, or only remember / correspond through Eru?
  • Is Erudite a permanent loan, a larva of the city, or a person who once lived in Ird?
  • When memory returns, does Soralyon already know — or is this the first time the Mystic Angel’s cleric can pray the truth?

Lithrei Woeborn — The unfinished student

Samsaran-mixed Human · Oracle (Life) / Investigator · Haunted
Dorm: Noxolo · Faculty: Ahassunu (Zuma secondary)

Relationships

  • Noxolo — peer who already lives next door to spirits (dead-bird augury, graveyard cloves, fugitive-from-a-planned-life). Shared language; fashion + omen. Not the passenger. Posture A: she notices dual-presence, chooses curiosity + discretion. Rare spikes where her augury channels the passenger’s mood without her knowing.
  • Ahassunu — vivid past, foggy present. Institutional memory that slips; perfect for scrubbed student-file leads.
  • Zuma (light) — “the school is hiding something”; useful, exhausting, sometimes right.
  • Anchor Root (light) — shared bone/spirit texture with Noxolo; do not re-primary.

The truth GM only

The spirit that rides Lithrei is a former Magaambya student who died badly on campus — neglect, cover-up, or “for the good of the school.” Not Uduak (rhyme, don’t merge). Healer monks contained the haunt-echo; acceptance brought the problem home. Someone at the school may have arranged the letter quietly — soft implication late, not a Book 1 neat culprit.

Session 1 He received the letter caged mid-fugue and woke up already at school. He supports the monks with emotional stability and has healed their wounds in return.

One-line pitch

Magaambya’s unfinished pastoral care, walking — a haunted Life oracle who is the school’s buried student problem wearing a badge.

Yggostrus — The sealed dragon

Dragonet (Homing Drake) · Summoner (Dragon eidolon, acid) · Apsu · cursed former dragon
Dorm: Esi Djana · Faculty: Session 1 druid mentor + Koride Ulawa (AP favorite)

Relationships

  • Esi Djana — star pupil who organizes the cohort and expects contribution. Speaks status/competence. Pressure surface for “I don’t need communities.”
  • Druid mentor Session 1 — bantery recruiter who found him ~100 years into the curse; wants him whole. Beloved teacher — do not overwrite.
  • Koride Ulawa — dangerous fascination; treats him as marvel/specimen. Indulges narcissism. Book 6: he is the PC who speaks first in the Koride parley.
  • Okoro (light) — low-stakes “court” via game night. Ignaci (light) — transactional cynic mirror (dangerous friend). Avoid Chizire-as-primary (enables detachment).

The truth GM only

The dragon eidolon is the rest of him — sealed true shape, not a separate pet. The curse split him. Breaking it power-first means reabsorbing into solitary tyrant; Magaambya-way means integration / stewardship without erasing the cohort.

GM rule: Progress toward unbinding advances when he acts through others (Aid, shared ritual, cohort risk on his behalf, accepting help he didn’t control). Public face: old dragon-magic needs a court. Underneath: the split won’t rejoin for someone who still intends to be alone.

Destination

Still a narcissistic dragon — but his hoard is people, and he would rather die than admit that in those words. Not “humble team player.”

Pedagogy (don’t sermonize)

Session 1 already showed the on-ramp: “You have use for me right now, so it's in my best interest.” Move him: use them → need them → owe them → choose whether “full strength” means alone.

Speak his language: court / retainers / territory / investment — not “friends” and “belonging.” Emerald Boughs pitch: “a dragon without a flight is just a lizard with opinions.”

Anya — The wild that enrolled

Versatile Human · Summoner (Beast eidolon) · Feral Child
Dorm: Anchor Root · Faculty: Takulu Ot

Relationships

  • Anchor Root — shy Rain-Scribe bonekeeper; lives between wildcraft and school. Eidolon may warm to them first. Quiet belonging buddy.
  • Takulu Ot — first adult who treats her as a student among people. Belonging curriculum in human form.
  • Tzeniwe (light) — soft pack/family practice; don’t steal Cy’An’s kitchen primary.

The truth GM only

The beast eidolon is a Magaambya-native wild spirit (Rain-Scribe / campus genius-loci fragment) that chose a feral child as its way into the school’s human community. Surface: Anya brought the wild to school. Truth: the wild enrolled itself through her. Belonging was the mission the whole time.

Not evil, not using her. Warm and inviting — opposite temperature from Lithrei’s passenger. Contrast with Yggostrus: organic invitation vs curse-split.

Session 1 Only friend is the eidolon; wilderness mail contacts; told Ot she wants greater connection to the bond and to nature.

One-line pitch

Campus ecology recruited a child so the Magaambya’s living wild could learn human community — and give her more than one friend.

Optional harder rhyme

Vesicant Egg pull vs eidolon pull as rival magnets on the same land (pests to feed a sealed king vs child to feed a living school). Reveal-optional; mostly GM compass for why she hates insect-wrongness on campus.

Cy’An Copperbottom — Provider-as-identity

Irongut Goblin · Inventor (Construct) / Wizard Dedication · Cook · they/them
Dorm: Tzeniwe · Staff: Lumusi Yao (Takulu secondary)

Relationships

  • Lumusi Yao — head chef who can put them on shift and send them home early (“Kitchen’s covered. Sit.”). Mentor for providing and for resting.
  • Tzeniwe — stress-baker; peer over-provider. Kitchen ↔ kitchen; mirror for “I must fix everyone.”
  • Takulu Ot (light) — studies how to teach. Voice for helping ≠ doing it for them (Session 1 already planted this).
  • Chizire (light) — Cascade grease / dorm still. Not the growth spine.

The wound GM only

Worth = usefulness. Cy’An is already good at care. The flaw is that when they cannot provide — no soup angle, no fix, empty hands — the self blanks. Remittances, shifts, and intervening mid-test are love language and armor.

Session 1 “I will provide”; stipend home immediately; jumped into Ot’s test while knowing it was cheating (“I need to let people fail on their own, I know”); bone-spirit rite with no cooking angle → “I did jack shit… What's soothing to my spirit is chicken noodle soup”; family-scale care, not civic yet (“I ain't got a big community…”).

Destination

Still a provider — remittances, meals, gadgets — but can receive care, sit with someone else’s struggle, and belong at Magaambya on a day they “did jack shit.” Teaching > fixing. Never stop being a cook-inventor; stop being a vending machine with anxiety.

How to teach it (don’t sermonize)

  • Put them where providing is impossible, wrong, or already covered.
  • Have people value them when they fail to provide.
  • Make teaching the higher form of providing (Takulu / Book 3).
  • Let home love them for showing up, not only for coin (letters evolve).

Speak their language: shift, portion, recipe, who’s hungry — then widen “hungry” to dignity, learning, rest.

Lethal AoE / nonlethal gremlins stay party table risk and a soft Uduak rhyme (“permanent fix”), not Cy’An’s character thesis.

Recurring devices

  • Empty shift — Lumusi/Tzeniwe/cohort covers their job; can they stay out?
  • Useless scene — no material fix; score whether they stay.
  • Taught meal — someone else cooks with their method.
  • Receive tokens — gifts unearned by labor; deflection early, acceptance late.
  • Remittance reframed — letters: thanks for coin → miss you → proud you left.
